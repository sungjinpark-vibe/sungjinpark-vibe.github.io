LoL Player Type — Research Introduction

A non-commercial academic study of laner behavioral archetypes in League of Legends, using the official Riot Games API.

1. Purpose & necessity

Each lane role in League of Legends demands a distinct pattern of play, and even within the same tier and role, players differ in how they control vision, farm, fight, contest objectives, and support their team. These differences form recurring behavioral archetypes that are not visible in simple win-rate or aggregate statistics.

Understanding these archetypes — and how they change across skill tiers and across regions — matters because it reveals the concrete behaviors that separate skill levels, informs coaching and learning, and gives an objective, data-driven picture of how the game is actually played worldwide. Answering this requires per-player behavioral data sampled consistently across the entire ranked ladder and every region, which is only obtainable through the Riot API at a production scale.

2. Research method

  • Data window: the 2025 competitive splits — Split 1 and Split 2.
  • Sampling: a random sample of 300 laners for each division (sub-tier), each lane, in every region — i.e. across Iron IV → Challenger, for TOP / JUNGLE / MIDDLE / BOTTOM / UTILITY, in all Riot regions (KR, NA, EUW, EUNE, LAN, LAS, BR, OCE, JP, and others).
  • Features: each player is summarized from recent Ranked Solo/Duo (queue 420) matches into behavioral metrics — vision score, KDA, creep score, damage to champions, turret damage, crowd-control time, and assists.
  • Analysis: metrics are normalized and, per role, reduced and grouped with unsupervised clustering (e.g. K-means with silhouette evaluation) to derive behavioral archetypes, whose prevalence is then compared across tiers and regions.
  • APIs used: LEAGUE-V4, MATCH-V5, SUMMONER-V4 (queue 420 only).

All results are reported in aggregate; PUUIDs are used only as transient lookup keys and are never published.

3. Positive impact on League of Legends R&D

A clear, quantitative map of how play styles differ by tier and region can support Riot's own research and development:

  • Balance: objective evidence of how roles and behaviors shift with skill, useful for champion and system balancing.
  • Matchmaking & retention: behavioral archetypes can inform matchmaking quality and help identify play patterns linked to engagement.
  • Player growth & coaching: showing players the measurable behaviors that distinguish higher tiers gives concrete, per-role paths to improve — aligned with Riot's goal of helping players get better and track growth.
  • Regional insight: cross-region comparison highlights how the meta and play styles differ globally.

The project aims to be a constructive, policy-compliant resource that benefits the wider League of Legends ecosystem.

4. References

This study builds on prior work applying data clustering to learner and game-player behavioral patterns:

  1. Park, S., & Kim, S. (2022). Learning Performance Styles in Gamified College Classes Using Data Clustering. Sustainability, 14(23), 15574. — K-means clustering with silhouette analysis to identify learners' performance styles.
  2. Park, S., & Kim, S. (2022). K-RBBSO Algorithm: A Result-Based Stochastic Search Algorithm in Big Data. Applied Sciences, 12(23), 12451. — a clustering-optimization algorithm (K-means-initiated rapid biogeography-based silhouette optimization).
  3. Park, S.-J., Kang, B.-S., Kim, S.-S., & Kim, S.-G. (2017). Data Clustering-Based Analysis of Player Types in Gamification Environments. Journal of Korea Game Society, 17(6). — K-means clustering and Bartle player typology to analyze game-player types.
